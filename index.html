About the Founder

British by birth.
American by choice.
Mission-driven.

After 25+ years at Microsoft — across Engineering, Product, and Support — James Whelan founded ChAImp to do something that hits different.

British by birth and American by choice, James spent 25 years solving problems inside Microsoft — for enterprise customers and the teams who served them. He now brings that same rigor, reliability, and pragmatism to community-level nonprofits — organizations that know technology could transform their impact, but need a trusted partner to get them there.

ChAImp is his give-back. For many of the organizations he works with, the engagement is entirely pro bono. Built with whatever technology serves the mission best — because the outcome always matters more than the stack.

Those who've worked alongside James will tell you he also brings something the tech industry often forgets — laughter and levity. He considers that just as important as the code.

The name ChAImp — pronounced "champ" — comes from somewhere personal — a reminder that championing others is the most important thing any of us can do.
